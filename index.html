§ I. Motivation

The Puzzle

§ I. · Motivation · Six minutes

A learned representation of a point cloud usually answers what is here? — class, label, embedding. It rarely answers where in the shape is here?

  • A vertex’s coordinate vector says nothing about its role in the shape.
  • Two combinatorially-identical patches at different curvatures look the same to a vanilla MLP.
  • Persistence already encodes shape globally — but global summaries flatten location.

What we want

§ I. · Motivation · Continued

A point-wise embedding that is aware of the topological neighbourhood it inhabits — a coordinate with a sense of place.

Fig. 1. — A printer’s flower, standing in for the figure your authors will provide on rendering.

Three desiderata:

  1. Equivariance under shape-preserving deformations.
  2. Discriminating power for points distinguishable by local topology.
  3. Cheap to compute at inference, even on graphs of millions of vertices.

The first two are theory questions; the third is engineering.

A coordinate that knows its place must remember, at minimum, the birth-merge structure of the components around it — a histogram of distances cannot.

§ II. Construction

Local atlas

§ II. · Construction · Architecture A

For each point we cut a small ball, run a filtration on it, and read off a coordinate from the resulting persistence diagram.

Calcutta, 16 May — The construction proceeds in three steps:

  1. Localise. Around vertex v, take a k-NN ball B_k(v) of radius r.
  2. Filter. Build the alpha filtration on B_k(v) up to scale r.
  3. Read. Extract the H_0 persistence diagram and embed it as a fixed-length vector \Phi(v) \in \mathbb{R}^d.

The resulting map v \mapsto \Phi(v) is the PLACE coordinate of v.

A theorem we can prove

§ II. · Construction · Theory

Let X, Y be two finite metric spaces, and let f \colon X \to Y be an \varepsilon-isometry. Then for every v \in X and every choice of vectorisation \nu that is L-Lipschitz on \mathcal{D}^{H_0}, \bigl\| \Phi_\nu(v) - \Phi_\nu(f(v)) \bigr\|_2 \;\leq\; 2 L \varepsilon.

The constant is sharp: equality is attained on the unit-circle pair at r = \pi/2.

A theorem we cannot

§ II. · Construction · Theory, continued

The H_0-only variant is provably blind to certain H_1-distinguishable pairs. Architecture B addresses this — at a price.

  • Architecture A (H_0 only): cheap, Lipschitz-stable, blind to loops.
  • Architecture B (H_0 \oplus H_1): ~3× more expensive, captures cycle birth, no closed-form stability bound.

The empirical question — does the loss of theory hurt? — is the subject of § III.

A denser look

§ III. · Experiments · In detail

ask SM about Joint-4 ablations — does H_1 help at all here?

The headline numbers come from the same architecture, the same five seeds, and the same hold-out split as the published baseline. Differences are not from hyperparameter retuning — we deliberately froze every choice that could otherwise be argued to favour PLACE.

The Joint-4 result is the one that surprised us. Naive theory predicts 50% on this synthetic, because all four classes share the same global H_1 signature. Architecture A nevertheless reaches 98.3%, indicating that the birth-merge structure of H_0 is what carries the angular-density information — a finding we did not anticipate when we wrote § II.

The MUTAG and NCI1 results are more conventional. They confirm that adding a topological coordinate to a vanilla GNN improves classification by a few points, in line with the persistence-on-graphs literature.
